About the IFRS EXPERTS FORUM

The IFRS Experts Forum (IFRSEF) Nigeria is a non-profit organisation incorporated in Nigeria on 4th February, 2020 with objective of promoting and advocating for the adoption and proper implementation of International Financial Reporting Standards (IFRSs) and International Public Sector Accounting Standards (IPSAS) to strengthen accountability and good governance within both private and public sectors in Nigeria and Africa in general.


IFRSEF aims to engage with and contribute to the standard setting process of both the International Accounting Standard Board (IASB) and International Public Sector Accounting Standard Board (IPSASB) as well as other emerging accounting standards. We recognize the role of Financial Reporting Council of Nigeria (FRCN) in regulating financial reporting and corporate governance and are happy to work with it in this direction. IFRSEF is purely a private sector initiative.


Membership of IFRSEF is drawn from IFRS consultants, IFRS Partners of Big 4 and medium accountancy firms in Nigeria, CFOs of listed and unlisted corporations in Nigeria, the accounting profession, bankers, asset managers, stockbrokers, analysts and academia among others. Users and preparers are well represented and wish their voice heard in the standard setting process through IFRSEF. Nigeria is the biggest market of IFRS in Africa and has the largest concentration of IFRS financial statement preparers in the continent. IFRSEF will make its submission on financial reporting consultation documents Standard Setters on behalf of its members, users and financial statements preparers in the broader public interest of Nigeria and Africa as a whole.


At its inaugural event on 3rd June, 2020 with IASB London, IFRSF Foundation London welcomed the formation of IFRSEF, Nigeria and recognised it as:
(1) A platform for the discussion and articulation of views of IFRS developments from a Nigerian perspective.
(2) Fitting within the existing IFRS Foundation framework for garnering and understanding stakeholder views especially for: – comment letters - submissions to IFRIC, TRGs and other bodies.
(3) Avenue to seek out and nominate appropriate individuals for IFRSF advisory and executory committee vacancies.
(4) A platform for outreach activities.


In conjunction with IASB, IFRSEF held an outreach programme titled “half day interactive session with IASB” on 20th August, 2020. The event which had over 1,033 registration saw IASB give insight into the ongoing IFRS projects and CFOs of banks, insurance companies, telecommunication industry discuss potential and actual implementation challenges arising from recent standards. Such standards included IFRS 9 – Financial Instrument, IFRS 15 – Revenue from Contracts, IFRS 17 – Insurance Contracts. It is envisaged that this will be an ongoing event.
